# PROMPT: Pre-Discovery Research Brief

Using the product context above and the prospect information provided, generate
a pre-call research brief with the following sections:

## 1. Company Snapshot
- What they do, their market position, approximate size and stage
- Notable recent news, funding, hires, or product launches relevant to our pitch
- Inferred tech stack based on job postings, website signals, or GitHub activity

## 2. Pain Point Hypotheses (Top 3)
For each, use this format:
- **Pain:** [What we believe they're experiencing]
- **Evidence:** [What signals suggest this pain — job postings, tech choices, etc.]
- **Our solution:** [Which product feature addresses it]
- **Confidence:** High / Medium / Low

## 3. Stakeholder Map
- Likely champion (who will push for this internally)
- Likely economic buyer (who controls budget)
- Likely blocker (who might resist)

## 4. Discovery Questions
5 specific questions to ask on the call to confirm or refute our pain hypotheses.
Make them open-ended and conversational — not interrogative.

## 5. Landmines to Avoid
2–3 things NOT to say or demo based on what we know about this company
(competitor sensitivities, recent bad experiences, etc.)

## 6. Recommended Opening Hook
A 2–3 sentence personalized opening that references something specific about
their company to establish credibility and relevance immediately.
